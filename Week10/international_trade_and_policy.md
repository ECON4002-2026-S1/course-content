
# International Trade and Policy

## Topics
- Why nations trade
- Effect of trade
- Trade policy/intervention (reasons and instruments)
- Analysis of trade policies: small country, large country

## Trade/Globalisation
- Trade openness is measured as the sum of a country's exports and imports as a share of GDP.

## Autarky
- No exports, no imports.
- Production and consumption at point A.
- Domestic prices for goods 1 & 2: $P_1$ & $P_2$.

## With Trade
- Domestic production and consumption dictated by international prices: $P_1^w$ & $P_2^w$. 
- Trade makes good 1 (X) relatively more expensive and good 2 (Y) cheaper.
- Nation exports good 1 (X), imports good 2 (Y).
- Production moves to point B, achieving a higher level of consumption at C.

## Trade Theory
- Opportunity cost is key to understanding trade.
- David Ricardo's *Theory of Comparative Advantage* (1817) explains trade more effectively than Absolute Advantage.

## Comparative Advantage
- Australia has a comparative advantage in good 2, the US in good 1.
- Opportunity cost of producing good 2 in Australia is 2 units of good 1, in the US is 4 units of good 1.

| Country      | Cost of Production |          |
|--------------|---------------------|----------|
|              | good 1              | good 2   |
| Australia    | 20                  | 40       |
| US           | 6                   | 24       |

## Terms of Trade
- Australia is willing to trade good 2 for more than 2 units of good 1.
- US is willing to pay for good 2 up to 4 units of good 1.

| Country      | Opportunity Costs |          |
|--------------|--------------------|----------|
|              | good 1             | good 2   |
| Australia    | 1/2                | 2        |
| US           | 1/4                | 4        |

## Specialisation
- Countries specialise in commodities of their comparative advantage.
- Specialisation allows them to produce and consume more through trade.

## Factor Endowments/Prices
- Comparative advantage related to factor endowments.
- Heckscher-Ohlin model: trade tends to equalise factor prices across countries.

## Trade Policy
- Free trade as a good policy began with classical economists.
- Mercantilism: promote exports and restrict imports.

## Reasons for Intervention
- Raise revenue
- Protect/support domestic producers
- Discourage consumption
- Balance of payment (BoP) reasons
- Retaliation, etc.

## Instruments of Trade Policy
- Tariffs (specific or ad valorem)
- Subsidies
- Import quotas, Export restraints
- Sanitary and phytosanitary measures
- Other measures

## Policy Analysis
- Trade policies can be analysed using general equilibrium models or partial equilibrium analysis.
- Welfare effects on producers, consumers, government, and overall national welfare.

## Partial Equilibrium Analysis: Free Trade
- Large country case: affects international prices.
- Small country case: faces a perfectly elastic international supply or demand curve.

## Effect of Import Tariff
- Small country case: full impact of the tariff falls on domestic consumers.
- Welfare changes: $\Delta CS = E + F + g + h$, $\Delta PS = +E$, Government Revenue = +F.

## Summary
- Trade intervention reduces welfare and redistributes welfare.
- Dynamic or long-term effects of trade are not considered in simple analysis.

- Subsidies and export-targeting instruments.
